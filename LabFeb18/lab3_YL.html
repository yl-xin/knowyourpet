My Favorate Places:

Chongqing

Chongqing is my hometown which is located at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers in southwestern China. It's very beaustiful. It's famous for its hot weather and hot food.

Chongqing was a municipality during the Republic of China (ROC) administration, serving as its wartime capital during the Second Sino-Japanese War (1937–1945). The current municipality was recreated on 14 March 1997 to help develop the central and western parts of China. It's the best place in the world.

Toronto

Toronto, the capital of the province of Ontario, is a major Canadian city along Lake Ontario’s northwestern shore.It's a dynamic metropolis with a core of soaring skyscrapers, all dwarfed by the iconic, free-standing CN Tower. Toronto also has many green spaces, from the orderly oval of Queen’s Park to 400-acre High Park and its trails.

My Favorate Meals:

Hot Pot

Chongqing Hot Pot is super spicy. The way of eating hot pot is put food in hot pot, wait for the food to cook, when the food is ready, dip them in sesame oil and eat.

ChongQing Liuyishou Hot Pot is more than a long and difficult to pronounce name; it's also one of the most popular hot pot chain restaurants from China. The average price is about 20$ per person.Chinese hot pot is a glorious communal feast and is easy to prepare with the right equipment and ingredients. Try it later! Make sure you order slightly spicy pot.

Beef Pho

A comforting richly seasoned beef broth is ladled over rice noodles and thinly sliced beef. Add hot sauce and plum sauce to taste and top with cilantro, basil, lime juice and bean sprouts
